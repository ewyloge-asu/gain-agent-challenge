# Investigation Brief

*Case file seed · Education lobbying investigation · demonstrates the Case File skill structure*

## The question

How does money shape federal education policy — who lobbies, who pays whom, and does
behavior track funding? Framed for this corpus: follow lobbying pressure and disclosed
contributions through the 2022–2026 education-policy fights (school choice, the Department
of Education restructuring, research-funding cuts).

## Scope

- **Corpus:** Senate LDA filings + LD-203 contribution reports, House LDA filings,
  congressional press releases. 2022 – 2026 Q1.
- **Frame:** process, disclosure, and mechanics — not whether any policy is good.

## What counts as a finding (the bar)

A finding must be (a) accurate and traceable to specific records, (b) of genuine public
interest — undisclosed relationships, timing anomalies, spending patterns — and (c) survive
the caveats in `workflow.md` (spend is not issue-specific; covered positions are
self-disclosed; correlation is not causation).

## How the investigation runs

The reproducible analysis workflow is documented in **`workflow.md`** (adapted from the
team's Methodology & Replication Guide). Every thread and finding in this case file names
the workflow step and the records that produced it, so an editor can re-run the work.

## Two illustrative outcomes

This seed shows two places the same workflow can land:

1. **A deep, near-confirmed story** — the ECCA revolving-door and contribution trail
   (`threads/T-0001-ecca-revolving-door.md`, `findings.yaml`).
2. **A board of open leads** — six story leads from the landscape analysis
   (`threads/T-0002` … `T-0007`), each awaiting reporting.
