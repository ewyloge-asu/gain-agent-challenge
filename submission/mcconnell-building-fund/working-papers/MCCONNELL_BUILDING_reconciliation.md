# The "Mitch McConnell Building" fund — full-period reconciliation (2022–2025)

**Question:** Get the complete list of everyone who funded the Republican Party of Kentucky building fund
(the "Mitch McConnell Building") from the real outside record over the *whole* period, line it up against
who disclosed the gift on their own federal lobbying forms, show exactly who reported and who didn't — and
where a donor apparently should have disclosed federally and didn't, check whether the law required it.

**Bottom line (revised — the single quarter badly understated it):** Over three years the fund took in
**~$4.43 million from about 22 named donors** (plus 16 small gifts), almost all corporations with lobbying
interests. Of that, only **four donors — Pfizer, AT&T, Microsoft, Delta ($1.475M)** — disclosed the gift on
their federal LD-203 lobbying reports. **Thirteen other donors that are themselves active federal lobbying
registrants — about $2.12M — did not.** A further ~$0.83M came from donors with no federal lobbying footprint
(so no federal duty), including the second-largest donor of all. Whether the 13 registrants were *required*
to disclose turns on one unsettled reading of the statute — so this is a strong *question for a lawyer and
for the companies*, not a finding of a violation. What changed from the one-quarter look: the gap went from
3 companies / $500k to **13 companies / ~$2.1M.**

## The three-way split

- **Disclosed on their federal LD-203 (honorary "he" item): 4 donors, $1,475,000** — Pfizer, AT&T, Microsoft,
  Delta. Cross-validation is clean: AT&T's $300k state total equals its $300k federal ($100k ×3, 2022–24);
  Microsoft $125k = $125k; Pfizer $1M = $1M. (Delta shows $50k in the state record but two $50k entries
  federally — a likely original+amendment duplicate.)
- **Federal lobbying registrants that gave but did NOT disclose a building gift: 13 donors, ~$2,122,500** —
  Altria $300k, MetLife $300k, Verizon $300k, Brown-Forman $300k, Boeing (PAC) $250k, Beam Suntory $200k,
  Sazerac $150k, Comcast $100k, Churchill Downs $100k, Airbnb $50k, National Thoroughbred Racing Assn. $50k,
  Rescare/BrightSpring $12.5k, Toyota $10k. Two of these *did* disclose *other*, McConnell-specific honoraria
  on their LD-203s (Airbnb reported $1,500 to AEI in his honor; Boeing reported $50k to a Hudson Institute
  event in his honor) — they simply left the far larger building-fund gift off. (A Toyota Kennedy Center gift
  that lists McConnell among ~14 bipartisan honorees is excluded here as not "in his honor.")
- **Not federal LD-203 filers, so no federal duty: 6 lines, ~$829,145** — including the **second-largest
  donor of the entire project, NWO Resources ($500,000)**, a privately held company with no federal lobbying
  footprint in the data, plus the horse-racing names Jockey Club and Keeneland, an individual's trust, an
  individual attorney, and 16 small gifts.

The headline, restated cleanly: **of the ~$3.6M that came from entities which file federal lobbying reports,
only $1.475M (4 donors) shows up in the federal honorary record; ~$2.12M (13 donors) does not.**

## Did the law require the 13 to disclose it? (statute, actually read)

**Verdict: genuinely unclear — leaning "disclosure appears to have been required" — a question for a lawyer,
not a violation I can assert.** The expansion *sharpens* rather than changes the earlier analysis: with 4
registrants treating the identical gift as reportable and 13 not, the industry itself is split, which is
exactly what makes it a live legal question.

**Governing provision:** 2 U.S.C. § 1604(d)(1)(E) (HLOGA 2007); definitions § 1602(4),(12). Read at
law.cornell.edu, as-of 2026-07-15. Walking the elements against the 13 registrants:

1. **Covered filer?** Met — all 13 are active federal LDA registrants filing LD-203s in this period.
2. **Funds contributed in the period?** Met — the state record dates and amounts establish this.
3. **To an entity "named for," or given "in recognition of," a covered official** (§ 1604(d)(1)(E)(ii))? The
   official prong is met — McConnell is a "Member of Congress," a "covered legislative branch official"
   (§ 1602(4)(A),(12)). The **"named for / in recognition of" prong is the crux**: the *building* is the
   "Mitch McConnell Building," but the *recipient entity* is the "RPK Building Fund," not literally named for
   him. Pfizer, AT&T, Microsoft, and Delta read this as reportable and filed it (honoree = McConnell); a
   filer could argue (E) reaches only a gift to an entity literally *named* for the official.
4. **FEC exception?** Does not apply — (E) exempts funds given to an FEC filer; this fund reports to the
   **Kentucky** Registry, not the FEC. (Consistently, the small FECA party gifts these same companies made
   *are* FEC-reported and correctly stay out of the honorary bucket.)

So all 13 are covered filers; the money moved; the recipient isn't an FEC filer; and the building bears a
sitting senator's name. If § 1604(d)(1)(E)(ii) reaches a gift to a fund for a building named for a senator —
as four peer companies concluded — the other 13 had the same duty and didn't file. If it reaches only gifts
to an entity literally named for the official, none were required (and the four over-disclosed). That one
interpretive question decides it.

**What would change the answer, donor by donor:** the entity-identity mismatches are the most live off-ramps
— **MetLife** (check writer "Metropolitan Life Insurance Co." vs registrant "MetLife Group, Inc."), **Boeing**
(gift via its PAC — though (E) expressly covers a "political committee established or controlled by" the
registrant, which cuts *toward* a Boeing duty), and **Rescare/BrightSpring**. Also: an official Secretary-of-
the-Senate/House-Clerk interpretation of (E); or a disclosure filed outside this dataset. One timing note: the
most recent tranches (e.g., Altria's Q4-2025 gift) fall in the H2-2025 LD-203 period due Jan 30, 2026 — but
these donors show *zero* building items across *all* years, so earlier tranches went undisclosed regardless.

**For a lawyer to confirm:** does § 1604(d)(1)(E)(ii)'s "named for … or in recognition of" reach a corporate
gift to a state-party building fund whose headquarters is named for a sitting senator; and are these building
funds definitively not FEC registrants?

## Before any of this is used

No party has been contacted. A reporter should seek comment from the 13 registrants that didn't disclose
(why the building gift isn't on their LD-203s), from the four that did (how they concluded it was reportable),
and from the Republican Party of Kentucky and Sen. McConnell's office — and a lawyer should review the
§ 1604(d) reading before publishing any "should have disclosed" framing. RPK's spokesman has said the fund's
fundraising "was done by the book."
