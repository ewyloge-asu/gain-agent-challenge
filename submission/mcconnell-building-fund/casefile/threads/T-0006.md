## Hypothesis (confirmed as documented, with a genuine interpretive question)
The single largest honorary item in the entire 2022-2026Q1 corpus is State Farm Mutual Automobile
Insurance Co. reporting a $2,650,000 payment to Habitat for Humanity International, honoree
"Rep. Virginia Foxx," dated 2025-11-20 (uuid 91514e3d-...).

## Outside verification (this is why we don't trust the form alone)
Unlike the lead-1 fabricated filing, this money is REAL: State Farm publicly announced in June 2025 it
was renewing its 30-year Habitat partnership and increasing contributions to ~$2.6M for youth
engagement and resilient-housing programs (habitat.org newsroom, 2025). The dollar figure and the
State Farm->Habitat relationship check out.

## The reportable nuance
What is notable is not the gift's legality but the attribution: routine, company-wide corporate
philanthropy appears on a federal LOBBYING disclosure tagged "in honor of" one sitting House member
(Foxx chairs / has chaired Education & the Workforce and Rules). Either Foxx was formally honored in
connection with the gift (a 1604(d)(1)(E)(i)/(ii) event) or the honoree tag is a loose attribution.
Both readings are worth a question to State Farm and Foxx's office. No cap applies; not a violation.
