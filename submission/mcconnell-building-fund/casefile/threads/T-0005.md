## Hypothesis (confirmed)
Corporate lobbying interests routed honorary money to institutions named for, or in recognition of,
Senate Republican Leader Mitch McConnell — most vividly the Republican Party of Kentucky "Building
Fund," whose headquarters is the "Mitch McConnell Building."

## What's documented (F-006)
- Republican Party of Kentucky / McConnell-building cluster: 8 LD-203 'he' items, ~$1.525M, 4 funders:
  Pfizer $1,000,000 (2022-12-27), AT&T $100k x3 (2022-24), Microsoft $100k (2023) + $25k (2025),
  Delta $50k x2 (2022) — all with honoree = McConnell / RPK building.
- Broader "in honor of McConnell" corporate giving (excluding U of Louisville self-report): ~$1.81M
  across 28 items / 18 funders, also reaching think-tank galas (AEI, Hudson) and the JFK Center.
- Separately, University of Louisville self-reports ~$2.53M to the "McConnell Center" and
  "McConnell-Chao Archives" — that is the university disclosing its own endowment/archive spending on
  a named center, NOT corporate influence money; keep it distinct.

## Outside verification
Pfizer's $1M to the Republican Party of Kentucky Building Fund is independently confirmed by Kentucky
Lantern, WKMS, and Hoptown Chronicle (Jan 2023); the HQ is named the "Mitch McConnell Building"; press
also names Comcast, MetLife, AT&T, Altria, Delta as fund donors. The filings match reality.

## Legal status
Textbook 2 U.S.C. 1604(d)(1)(E)(ii) disclosures (money to an entity named for / in recognition of a
covered official). Legal and disclosed; no cap. Not a violation. See T-0004 legal note.
