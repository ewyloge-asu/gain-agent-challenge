## Hypothesis (documented; one open legal question)
The complete outside record of who funded the "Mitch McConnell Building" (Republican Party of Kentucky
Building Fund) diverges from the federal lobbying-disclosure record: half a million dollars visible in
Kentucky's state filings never appears in the donors' own federal LD-203 honorary reports.

## What's documented (F-008)
- KREF Q4-2022 building-fund report: 6 corporate donors, $1.65M — Pfizer $1M, MetLife $300k, Altria $100k,
  Comcast $100k, AT&T $100k, Delta $50k (source: Ky Lantern citing the KREF filing).
- Federal LD-203 (honorary/he) reconciliation:
  DISCLOSED: Pfizer $1M (uuid 7fcdd899), AT&T $100k (uuid 19b89a8f), Delta $50k (shown twice fed = likely
  original+amendment) => $1,150,000 disclosed.
  NOT disclosed: MetLife $300k, Altria $100k, Comcast $100k => $500,000 absent from federal honorary record.
- The three non-disclosers ARE active federal registrants (MetLife Group 564 LD-203 items, Altria 1,602,
  Comcast 3,186); their only KY federal items are ordinary FECA PAC gifts, not the building-fund checks.

## Legal question (checking-the-law; genuinely-unclear, leans "was required")
2 U.S.C. 1604(d)(1)(E)(ii) requires a registrant to disclose funds given to an entity "named for" a covered
official or "in recognition of" such official; FEC-reportable gifts are carved out. Elements: (1) covered
filer = MET (all three registrants); (2) funds disbursed = MET; (3) covered official = MET (McConnell is a
Member of Congress, 1602(4)(A)/(12)); the "named for/in recognition of" prong is the crux — the building is
named for McConnell but the recipient entity is the "RPK Building Fund." (4) FEC exception does NOT apply —
the fund reports to the KY Registry, not the FEC. Pfizer + AT&T treated the identical gift as reportable and
filed it, which cuts toward "covered." Verdict: appears required, but a fair textual argument runs the other
way => question for a lawyer; assert only "possible." Would change: MetLife entity-identity mismatch;
official LDA guidance on (E); a filing outside this dataset.

## Framing rules
Do NOT assert a disclosure violation. Strongest statement: "$500k appears in the state record but not in the
federal honorary record; whether the law required these registrants to disclose it turns on an unsettled
reading of 1604(d)(1)(E) and is a question for a lawyer." Seek comment from all parties first.
