## Finding 1 (headline): Lobbyist money pools around the committee gatekeepers

**Claim.** In 2025, registered-**lobbyist** LD-203 contributions concentrated on the members
who control whether legislation moves — committee chairs/ranking members and floor
leadership.

**Evidence [corpus].** Top 2025 recipients of contributions filed by *lobbyists*
(honoree → member `bioguide`, resolved at 81.8% coverage after seeding the member table from
the authoritative congress-legislators roster and resolving campaign-committee honorees
like "Guthrie for Congress" to their member):

| Rank | Member | Lobbyist $ (2025) | Distinct registrants |
|---:|---|---:|---:|
| 1 | Brett Guthrie (R-KY) | $694,295 | 177 |
| 2 | Jason Smith (R-MO) | $674,775 | 119 |
| 3 | Mark R. Warner (D-VA) | $632,009 | 164 |
| 4 | Steve Scalise (R-LA) | $508,265 | 67 |
| 5 | Mike Johnson (R-LA) | $473,209 | 43 |
| 6 | Thom Tillis (R-NC) | $467,203 | 132 |
| 7 | Tom Cole (R-OK) | $465,971 | 97 |
| 8 | Mike Carey (R-OH) | $457,648 | 101 |
| 9 | John Thune (R-SD) | $452,805 | 100 |
| 10 | Hakeem Jeffries (D-NY) | $426,950 | 57 |

**External verification [corpus + outside data].** Cross-referenced against the public
`unitedstates/congress-legislators` roster (shipped snapshot `assets/committees.json`), the
leaderboard is dominated by **committee chairs / ranking members** — Guthrie (Chair, House
Energy & Commerce), Jason Smith (Chair, Ways & Means), Tom Cole (Chair, Appropriations),
Warner (Ranking, Senate Banking; Intelligence), J. French Hill (Chair, Financial Services,
#11 at $425K) — plus floor leaders (Speaker Johnson, Majority Leaders Scalise and Thune,
Minority Leader Jeffries).

**The discipline that keeps this honest.** The defensible signal is **which bills a member
chooses to sponsor** (discretionary, and it tracks their committee/donor areas), *not* floor
votes: gatekeepers vote with their party ~97–99% of the time, so vote-level "donor alignment"
is confounded by party and is **not** claimed. Money → *sponsorship* is the pattern; money →
*votes* is not asserted. Documented ≠ causation.

**The denominator [corpus + outside data, Tier 2].** Grounded against each member's FEC
receipts (connector `enrich-finance`, shipped snapshot), registered lobbyists are on the
order of **13–18% of the entire campaign haul** for the money-committee chairs (Guthrie
~17%, Jason Smith ~15%, Carey ~18%, Cole ~13%), versus a few percent for floor leaders who
raise far more overall (Johnson ~3%, Jeffries ~3%). (Free FEC key unlocks a live refresh;
the snapshot reproduces it offline.)

*Denominator method note:* the numerator is 2025 LD-203 lobbyist contributions; the
denominator is each member's total receipts for their **current FEC election cycle**
(House 2026 cycles begin Jan 2025, so windows overlap but are not identical; Senate
cycles span up to six years — Warner's covers 2021–2026, Thune's is the 2028 cycle). The
share is therefore an order-of-magnitude grounding, strongest for House members, not an
exact same-window ratio; cross-chamber comparisons should be read loosely. Cycle and
coverage dates for every member are recorded in the snapshot (`assets/fec_totals.json`).

**Reproduce:**
```
xref.py gatekeeper --filer lobbyist --year 2025 --top 12
connector.py annotate-gatekeeper --year 2025 --filer lobbyist --top 20   # committee roles
connector.py enrich-finance --year 2025 --filer lobbyist --top 12        # FEC denominator
```

## Finding 2 (headline): Foreign companies under U.S. scrutiny hired insiders, disclosed

**Claim [corpus + context].** When foreign companies faced adverse U.S. government action they
ramped lobbying and retained people with insider government credentials — all disclosed, but
newsworthy.

- **Tencent (China).** Reported lobbying **jumped ~5× — $800K (2024) → $4.04M (2025),
  both amendment-aware corpus totals re-derived this session** (the #6 real 2025 client by
  de-duplicated income; registrants 2 → 6) as it fought a DoD "Chinese military company"
  designation *[context]*. Registrant **John McEntee** carries the disclosed covered position
  "Officer in the Executive Office of the President" and registered on the single issue
  **"Erroneous trade restriction designation"** — `filing_uuid
  b8b09920-a089-4e40-badb-eaaa93cf6a8d` *[corpus]*.
- **FARA angle [corpus + outside data].** The firms hold FARA registrations, but **McEntee
  does not appear in FARA**, so his Tencent work runs under the lighter LDA regime — the
  LDA-vs-FARA election that is a recognized compliance gray area. Confirming the precise
  foreign principal needs FARA's foreign-principals dataset (documented next step).

**Legal status.** Disclosed and presumptively lawful; flagged as a **question for a lawyer**
(cooling-off compliance would need departure dates not in this corpus), not asserted as a
violation. See `checking-the-law` verdict `legal_checks/mcentee_fara.md`.

**Reproduce:** `ingest.py --years 2024 2025 --datasets senate`; `xref.py client "TENCENT"`
(2024 vs 2025 income); `xref.py lobbyist "John McEntee"`; `connector.py fara`.

## Finding 3 (flag): The disclosure system has no gatekeeper of its own (Loc Nation)

**Claim + LEGAL/ETHICS FLAG.** The naive "biggest 2025 lobbying client" is fictional. A
single filer — **"State of Loc Nation Global Public Benefit Corporation" / "LOC Community
Association" (Dover, DE)** — filed **four 2025 quarterly reports each claiming $20,000,000 of
income = $80,000,000** (the #1 income outlier, **15.6×** the next real client, Qualcomm at
$5.13M). The filings are not lobbying: they describe pseudo-legal reparations claims tied to
HR 40 and a self-issued "Loc Nation Dollar."

**The number, reconciled [corpus].** A naive sum of all **11** Loc Nation filings reads
**$180M** — but 7 of them are quarterly *amendments*. Amendment-aware de-duplication (latest
filing per registrant/client/quarter) gives the defensible **$80M across four quarters**.
Even $80M is nominal, not real money. (This corrected an amendment double-count that had
three earlier solo analyses reporting $60M / $80M / $180M.)
`filing_uuid 4d5b1cb0-0971-43b8-958b-d260e2be8af1` (+ others). Verify: `review.py "<prov>"`.

**Why it matters.** (1) The LDA system performs no substantive validation — anyone can file
anything, and it pollutes any naive aggregate. (2) Flagged to the panel as a **possible
misuse of a federal disclosure system**. Prior public notice exists (LegiStorm, Aug 2025), so
this is the fuller, reproducible, full-year, amendment-corrected version — not a first
discovery. See `checking-the-law` verdict `legal_checks/loc_nation.md`.

**Reproduce:** `xref.py anomaly --year 2025 --factor 5`.

## Capability demonstrated (not claimed as a finding): coordinated-messaging detection

The coordination detector finds verbatim talking points reused across many offices in a tight
window and scores them so *targeted* campaigns separate from ordinary boilerplate. On the 2025
press corpus (48,317 releases) it surfaced, e.g., a **9-member bipartisan cluster** (span 2
days, specificity 2) reusing an identical Center for American Progress statistic while
reintroducing the **American Dream and Promise Act** (Feb 2025), and a 6-member Maryland
delegation cluster on a firefighter-grant announcement. This is a reusable **capability** more
than a standalone scoop. **Reproduce:** `detect_coordination.py clusters --year 2025 --min-members 6`.

## Cold thread (documented, not a story)

Whether firms report different dollars to the two chambers for the same engagement:
**re-verified on this session's full 2025 re-ingest** — 108,518 House filings bridged to
Senate filings (98.3% linked), **36,643 comparable engagement-periods, zero gaps over
$100K**. A clean dead end, reported so it is not re-chased.

*A methods catch worth recording:* the first pass showed 19 apparent six-figure gaps —
every one a **quarterly amendment artifact** (e.g. $1.2M originally filed in both chambers,
later amended to $120K in both; the naive join compared one chamber's original against the
other's amendment). Making the comparison amendment-aware on *both* chambers — the same
discipline that corrected Loc Nation's $180M — collapsed all 19 to zero. Cross-chamber
consistency claims made without amendment de-dup will manufacture phantom discrepancies.
**Reproduce:** `ingest.py --years 2025 --datasets house`; `resolve_entities.py`;
`xref.py mismatch --year 2025 --min-gap 100000`.
