notes
Hypothesis (from the OIG report surfaced at acquisition): some hospitals bill simple
pneumonia at the highest-severity DRG (193, "with MCC") at rates far above peers —
the exact pattern HHS-OIG recommends targeting for up-coding review.

Evidence so far (CMS DY2024 public-use data, providers with ≥50 pneumonia discharges):
national median highest-severity share is **74.4%** (artifact-free); the top artifact-free
outliers bill **~90–92%** — e.g. CHI St Vincent Hot Springs (AR) 92.4% of 157, UW Health
(IL) 92.3% of 155, Texas Health Huguley (TX) 92.2% of 154. Full ranked list with method +
caveats: `../leads_upcoding_signal.json`.

Methods note (human judgment): the naive first pass returned a wall of 100% shares — a
**CMS small-cell suppression artifact** (lower-severity rows with <11 discharges are
withheld), not a signal. 120 providers flagged as artifacts and excluded.

These are **leads, not findings**: severity mix can reflect real patient acuity
(transfer centers, teaching hospitals). Next steps: case-mix adjustment, multi-year
trend per OIG's method, and the DRG 871/872 (sepsis) pair.
