## Hypothesis (confirmed)
The federal lobbying-disclosure system has no gatekeeper: it accepts and publishes self-certified
filings with no content check on the way in, and the government enforces the law almost entirely
against non-filers on the way out. A single fabricated filing proves both ends.

## What's documented (see findings F-001..F-004)
- A fictitious 'sovereign nation' reported $80M in 2025 lobbying fees (F-001).
- That figure is ~21x the next real client by income and exceeds the largest real in-house budget,
  US Chamber ~$70.3M (F-002).
- It distorted national totals (~1.3% of all reported lobbying dollars; 2.78% of income only) (F-002).
- The 'no dollar figure' gap is structural (~10-12% of activity reports every year) (F-003).
- A false filing is illegal in principle (2 U.S.C. 1606; maybe 18 U.S.C. 1001) but effectively
  unenforced; GAO shows enforcement aimed at non-filers, no documented false-content penalty (F-004).

## Framing rule (non-negotiable)
Story is about the SYSTEM, not the filer. Every legal hook needs intent to deceive; a sincere-belief
filer may be outside the statutes entirely — which is itself why the spotlight belongs on the absent
gatekeeper, not the individual. Assert only "possible, for a lawyer." No party contacted yet.
