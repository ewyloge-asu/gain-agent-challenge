# "Effectively unenforced" — now backed by the government's own numbers

*You wanted to stand behind that phrase with a real record, not just my reading of the statute.
There is one, and it's the government's own auditor. Below: what the record shows, what's
primary vs. secondary, and what I could NOT establish so you don't overstate it.*

---

## The source: GAO audits LDA enforcement every year, by law

Congress requires the **Government Accountability Office (GAO)** — the federal government's own
nonpartisan watchdog — to audit lobbying-disclosure compliance **annually**. The most recent is the
**2024 report, GAO-25-107523** (GAO's 18th annual review). It reports, from official U.S. Attorney's
Office (USAO) data, exactly how enforcement works and how much of it happens. I read it directly.

## What the record actually shows

**1. The entire enforcement pipeline is about *failing to file* — not false content.**
Over the ten filing years **2015 through 2024**, the Secretary of the Senate and the Clerk of the
House referred to the U.S. Attorney for D.C.:

- **3,566 referrals** for failing to file quarterly **LD-2** activity reports, and
- **2,000 referrals** for failing to file **LD-203** contribution reports.

That's **~5,566 referrals in a decade, every one for non-filing** (a lobbyist who didn't file at
all, or filed late). None are for a filer who submitted a false number. *(GAO-25-107523, Figures 11–12
and surrounding text.)*

**2. Even the "enforcement actions" are mostly letters and phone calls.** GAO states plainly that the
enforcement actions USAO takes to bring lobbyists into compliance **"include letters, emails, and
telephone calls."** Of the 3,566 LD-2 referrals, about **36% (1,290)** eventually filed; about
**63% (2,244)** were still **pending** as of December 2024 — because USAO "could not locate the
lobbyists" or got no response. In other words, most referrals are never actually resolved.

**3. Actual penalties are vanishingly rare, and aimed at repeat non-filers.** In all of **2024**, the
Department of Justice took exactly **one** civil enforcement action under the LDA — a **$65,000
settlement** (plus a promise to retire from lobbying) against a lobbyist USAO had flagged as a
**"chronic offender"** (someone who **repeatedly failed to file**). It took **zero criminal**
enforcement actions that year. *(GAO-25-107523, "Status of Civil and Criminal Enforcement Actions.")*

**4. No criminal prosecutions for the disclosure-fraud provision — ever.** As of December 2024, DOJ
**had not brought a single prosecution** under the 2019 JACK Act (which requires lobbyists to disclose
certain criminal convictions) since it took effect. And GAO records **no** criminal LDA prosecutions
in its recent reviews. Notably, GAO records USAO confirming it *could* act on a filing "with
misrepresentations" — it just hasn't. *(GAO-25-107523.)*

**5. The longer historical picture is the same — a handful of civil settlements, all for missing
reports.** Earlier GAO reports and reputable legal write-ups document that in the years after 2007
the total known monetary enforcement amounts to a **small handful** of civil settlements/judgments,
each against a lobbyist who **failed to file** — for example negotiated settlements of **$45,000
(2011)**, **$50,000 and $30,000 (2012)**, a **$200,000 default judgment (Dec 2013)**, and a
**$125,000 settlement (Aug 2015, "for missing LDA reports")**. I'm flagging these as *illustrative and
partly from secondary sources* (see caveat below) — but every documented action I found is a
non-filing case, none for false content.

## The line you can stand behind

> Over the decade 2015–2024, the Secretary of the Senate and Clerk of the House sent the U.S.
> Attorney some **5,566 enforcement referrals under the lobbying-disclosure law — every one for
> failing to file, none for false content** — and the office's "enforcement actions" were largely
> letters, emails and phone calls, with about **63% of referrals still unresolved**. Actual penalties
> are rare and reserved for chronic non-filers (a single **$65,000** civil settlement in 2024, and
> **zero criminal prosecutions**). The government's own auditor documents **no** case of anyone being
> penalized for putting a false figure into the record. *(Source: GAO-25-107523, 2024 Lobbying
> Disclosure audit.)*

That is a sourced, defensible foundation for "effectively unenforced" — and it's stronger than the
phrase, because it shows the enforcement machine isn't just small, it's **pointed at a different
problem** (non-filers) than the one your story is about (false content).

## Straight talk — what I did NOT establish (don't overstate these)

- **I do not have a single authoritative all-time count** of "total LDA enforcement actions, 1996–
  present." GAO reports year-by-year; the 2015–2024 referral totals and the 2024 action count are
  solid and primary. The pre-2015 settlement examples are from older GAO reports and law-firm
  summaries and should be **re-verified against the underlying GAO reports** before printing specific
  dollar figures.
- **The truly authoritative all-time number exists** and is worth requesting: under **2 U.S.C.
  § 1605(b)**, the Attorney General must report to Congress **every six months** the aggregate number
  of LDA enforcement actions and any sentences. Those DOJ semiannual reports (to the Senate Homeland
  Security/Judiciary and House Judiciary committees) are the primary record; obtaining a run of them
  would let you state an exact cumulative figure with total confidence.
- **"None for false content" is true of everything I found, but is a negative** — best framed as "no
  documented case of enforcement for a false filing," and confirmed on the record with USAO and via
  the § 1605(b) reports before publication.

## Bottom line for the story

The "no gatekeeper" thesis is now supported on both ends: **nothing checks a filing on the way in**
(a fabricated $80M posted and distorted the national total), and **almost nothing happens after** —
the government's own auditor shows enforcement is rare, mostly nudge-letters to non-filers, with no
documented penalty ever for false content. You can write "effectively unenforced" and point to GAO.
