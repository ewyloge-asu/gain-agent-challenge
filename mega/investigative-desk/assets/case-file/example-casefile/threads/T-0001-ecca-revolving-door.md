## Hypothesis

The ECCA — a $10B/yr federal school-voucher tax credit enacted inside the One Big
Beautiful Bill (H.R. 1, May 2025) — was advanced by a concentrated influence operation:
former Hill/White House staff who cycled through the revolving door, lobbied the same
lawmakers they once served, and whose firms/PACs contributed to those sponsors during the
same period.

## What's documented (see findings F-001/F-002/F-003)

- Network spend: $44.5M total in 2025 (up from $6.6M in 2024); education-specific $4–6M.
- Sponsors: Foxx (H.R. 833), Cassidy (S. 292), Walberg (markup chair), + Scott, Banks,
  Young, Jason Smith (Ways & Means, tax-credit jurisdiction).
- Revolving-door roster and contribution trail assembled from filings + LD-203.

## What's still open (why this is `chasing`, not `confirmed`)

- **Causation unproven.** Contribution + contact of the same member is documented; that
  the money moved a vote is a reporting question. Do not assert influence.
- **FEC gap.** LD-203 misses client, client-PAC, and executive money (Stride, NAPCS, AFC/
  Betsy DeVos orbit, Yes Every Kid's unreported donors). FEC cross-reference required.
- **Verify covered positions** individually (self-disclosed).
- **Votes/amendments** not yet pulled (Congress.gov): committee markup, H.R. 1 floor vote,
  S. 292 cosponsors vs. contribution list.
- **Comment** not yet requested from lobbyists/members/clients (standard questions drafted
  in the memo).
- **Bipartisan angle:** Daschle (D) lobbied for Stride on a GOP-advanced bill — call him.
